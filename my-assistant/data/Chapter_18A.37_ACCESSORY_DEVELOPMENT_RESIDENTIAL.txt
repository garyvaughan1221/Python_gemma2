The purpose of this Chapter is to identify certain uses and structures that may be established accessory to a residence and to establish standards and conditions for regulating such uses and structures.
A. In all zones, there shall be no limit as to the number of accessory uses allowed on a lot provided:
18A.37.020.A 1. The use meets all applicable development regulations; and
18A.37.020.A 2. The use is accessory to a lawfully established residence, except for structures listed in PCC 18J.17.020.E (18J.17.020.E) .
B. Structures typically accessory to a residence, such as garages or carports, greenhouses and storage buildings, may be permitted without a principal use up to a maximum of 576 total square feet. Such structures shall not be used as living accommodations. Fences and retaining walls shall not be considered a principal use and shall not be limited to the 576 square feet maximum when no principal use is located on site.
C. In residential zones, on lots of less than 1 acre in size, detached unoccupied accessory structures shall not exceed 2,000 total square feet. Shared garages or carports allowed in PCC 18J.17.020.E (18J.17.020.E) are exempt from the 2,000-square-foot size limit. See PCC 18A.37.120 (18A.37.120) for regulations pertaining to Accessory Dwelling Units.
D. All accessory uses must be customarily incidental and subordinate to the principal building or use of the lot upon which it is located.
E. At such time as the principal use or structure is discontinued, all accessory uses and structures to the principal use or structure shall also be discontinued or removed.
F. Where there is a question regarding the inclusion or exclusion of a particular accessory use within any zone classification, the Director shall have the authority to make the final determination. The determination shall be based upon the general standards of this Section and an analysis of the compatibility of the use or structural size and placement with consideration of the predominant surrounding land use pattern and with the permitted principal uses of the zone classification.
The following uses may be allowed accessory to a residence:
A. Decks and patios.
B. Greenhouse and landscape structures.
C. Carports or garages for the sole use of occupants of premises and their guests, attached or detached (without fee to guests), for storage of motor vehicles, boats, recreational vehicles, and/or planes.
D. Antennae and satellite dishes.
E. Recreational facilities and areas (indoor or outdoor), including swimming pools.
F. Docks and mooring facilities (see Title 18S (18S) PCC, Shoreline Development Policies and Regulations – Shorelines).
G. Retaining walls, free-standing walls, and fences.
H. Small-scale composting facility which meets State and local solid waste regulations.
I. Personal hobby activities, limited to uses and activities permitted to take place within a residence or residential accessory structure per appropriate building and fire codes.
18A.37.030.I 1. Personal hobby activities shall be undertaken for personal use only and shall not be used to generate income of any sort.
18A.37.030.I 2. Personal hobby activities may not include uses that together would constitute a dwelling unit.
J. Storage buildings for yard maintenance equipment and household goods.
K. Agricultural activities, such as gardens and orchards.
A. A Bed and Breakfast shall be allowed, on a parcel upon which the bed and breakfast proprietor resides, within a legally established single-family dwelling or accessory structure when the following criteria are met:
18A.37.040.A 1. Not more than five guest rooms are provided;
18A.37.040.A 2. Not more than ten travelers are lodged at any time;
18A.37.040.A 3. Lodging for each guest does not exceed two weeks;
18A.37.040.A 4. Compensation of any kind is paid for the lodging; and
18A.37.040.A 5. The B&B owner files an affidavit with Pierce County Planning and Public Works which indicates the location of the property and that the property will be used as a bed and breakfast.
B. A Vacation Rental (VR) is a short-term rental accommodation within a legally established single-family or accessory dwelling. Longer term rentals exceeding 30 days are not regulated under this Chapter. Vacation rentals shall be allowed when the following criteria are met:
18A.37.040.B 1. A total of five guest rooms may be provided.
18A.37.040.B 2. Two guests per bedroom, not to exceed a total of ten guests may be lodged at any time.
18A.37.040.B 3. Compensation of any kind is paid for the lodging.
18A.37.040.B 4. The VR owner or representative shall provide guests with information indicating the location of guest parking spaces.
18A.37.040.B 5. The VR owner or representative shall provide notification of the presence of the vacation rental business to all neighboring property owners directly adjacent to the vacation rental property.
18A.37.040.B 6. The property owner shall file a Vacation Rental Affidavit with the Pierce County Planning and Public Works Department which indicates the following:
18A.37.040.B 18A.37.040.B.6 a. The property owner's intent to use their residence as a Vacation Rental.
18A.37.040.B 18A.37.040.B.6 b. Contact information for either the owner, representative, or property management company including phone number and e-mail.
18A.37.040.B 18A.37.040.B.6 c. Listing of the internet site(s) where the Vacation Rental property is advertised.
18A.37.040.B 18A.37.040.B.6 d. The required neighborhood notification has been provided.
18A.37.040.B 7. A Good Neighbor brochure for short-term rentals is provided to each renter describing the appropriate etiquette of residing in a Vacation Rental.
C. If the standards described in either PCC 18A.37.040.A (18A.37.040.A) or B (18A.37.040.B) above are exceeded or otherwise cannot be met, the proposed use shall require a Conditional Use Permit.
Single-family residential uses located on lots greater than 2.5 acres in rural zone classifications shall be permitted to utilize a cargo container for storage purposes when a Level F1 Fence, per PCC 18J.15.040.H.5 (18J.15.040.H.5) , or Level L2 landscape buffer, per PCC 18J.15.040.H.2 (18J.15.040.H.2) , surrounds the cargo container(s), provided the cargo container shall not be visible from any County arterial road. A cargo container authorized pursuant to this subsection shall be painted and have any corporate identification, symbol or logos removed. The use of semi-trailers for storage purposes is not permitted.
A. Animals may be allowed, subject to the following requirements:
18A.37.060.A 1. Any combination of five dogs or cats that individually exceed seven months of age are permitted. Any combination of six or more dogs or cats that individually exceed seven months of age are permitted if allowed pursuant to PCC 18A.33.260 (18A.33.260) D., Animal Production, Boarding and Slaughtering and as shown on the appropriate Use Table and licensed pursuant to Chapter 5.24 (5.24) PCC.
18A.37.060.A 2. No person shall have, maintain, or possess any wolf, fox, chimpanzee, or other exotic, vicious, or poisonous animal or reptile except as set forth in Chapter 6.16 (6.16) PCC.
B. Urban Area Requirements.
18A.37.060.B 1. For lots less than one-half acre:
18A.37.060.B 18A.37.060.B.1 a. Two small livestock are allowed, all other livestock are prohibited; and
18A.37.060.B 18A.37.060.B.1 b. Five or fewer small animals, excluding dogs and cats allowed pursuant to PCC 18A.37.060.A.1 (18A.37.060.A.1) above, are allowed. Roosters, peacocks, and male turkeys are prohibited.
18A.37.060.B 2. For lots from one-half acre to less than 5 acres:
18A.37.060.B 18A.37.060.B.2 a. Two small livestock are allowed and other livestock shall not exceed 2 which are 12 months or more of age; and
18A.37.060.B 18A.37.060.B.2 b. Twelve or fewer small animals, excluding dogs and cats allowed pursuant to PCC 18A.37.060.A.1 (18A.37.060.A.1) above, are allowed per acre. Roosters, peacocks, and male turkeys are prohibited on lots less than one acre.
18A.37.060.B 3. For lots 5 acres or greater in size, there shall be no restriction on the number of livestock and small animals kept excluding dogs and cats allowed pursuant to PCC 18A.37.060 (18A.37.060) A.1 above.
18A.37.060.B 4. Enclosures such as, but not limited to, homes, garages, cages, barns, stables, and coops shall not be located in a front yard, and shall be set back as follows from adjacent residential property lines that are not held in common ownership:
18A.37.060.B 18A.37.060.B.4 a. 15 feet for poultry or rabbits;
18A.37.060.B 18A.37.060.B.4 b. 10 feet for other small animals and small livestock;
18A.37.060.B 18A.37.060.B.4 c. 45 feet for livestock; and
18A.37.060.B 18A.37.060.B.4 d. 75 feet for kennels and catteries.
18A.37.060.B 18A.37.060.B.4 e. For purposes of this Section, a fenced area where animals are free roaming is not considered an animal enclosure.
C. Rural Area Requirements.
18A.37.060.C 1. For lots less than one-half acre:
18A.37.060.C 18A.37.060.C.1 a. Two small livestock are allowed, all other livestock are prohibited; and
18A.37.060.C 18A.37.060.C.1 b. No more than 24 small animals, excluding dogs and cats allowed pursuant to PCC 18A.37.060 (18A.37.060) A.1 above.
18A.37.060.C 2. For lots one-half acre or greater in size, there shall be no restriction on the number of small livestock, livestock or small animals kept excluding dogs and cats allowed pursuant to PCC 18A.37.060 (18A.37.060) A.1 above.
18A.37.060.C 3. Enclosures such as, but not limited to, homes, garages, cages, barns, stables, and coops shall not be located in a front yard, and shall be set back as follows from adjacent residential property lines that are not held in common ownership:
18A.37.060.C 18A.37.060.C.3 a. 5 feet for poultry or rabbits;
18A.37.060.C 18A.37.060.C.3 b. 10 feet for other small animals and small livestock;
18A.37.060.C 18A.37.060.C.3 c. 45 feet for livestock; and
18A.37.060.C 18A.37.060.C.3 d. 75 feet for kennels and catteries.
18A.37.060.C 18A.37.060.C.3 e. For purposes of this Section, a fenced area where animals are free roaming is not considered an animal enclosure.
D. Best Management Practices on Lots Less Than One-Half Acre.
18A.37.060.D 1. Manure shall be collected and stored in covered containers. Animal manure shall not be deposited or allowed to accumulate in any ditch, ravine, stream, river, pond, marine water, or surface of the ground, or on any road right-of-way where it may become a nuisance or menace to health as determined by the Tacoma-Pierce County Health Department.
18A.37.060.D 2. All critical area buffers and setbacks, building department, solid waste, aquifer recharge and health department regulations apply.
A. Motor tracks may be allowed subject to the following restrictions, in addition to any applicable development regulations:
18A.37.070.A 1. Rural zones only;
18A.37.070.A 2. Parcels must be equal to or exceed 5 contiguous acres in size;
18A.37.070.A 3. Track shall be within an area not to exceed 2 acres in size;
18A.37.070.A 4. No portion of the usable track may be within 50 feet of any property line and no closer than 250 feet to a principal residential structure on any abutting property;
18A.37.070.A 5. The track shall only be utilized during daylight hours; no earlier than 8 a.m. and no later than 6 p.m. year round; and
18A.37.070.A 6. Use of the track is limited to the occupant(s) of the single-family residence.

The vehicle may exceed 18,000 pounds gross vehicle weight if under contract with local authorities, such as the Sheriff and/or State Patrol, to provide assistance to automobile accidents or provide assistance during inclement weather conditions.

A. Wind power electrical generation equipment meeting the following standards shall be allowed accessory to a Level 1 or Level 2 Single-Family Housing use:
18A.37.090.A 1. The equipment may not exceed more than one unit per principal residence, and shall have a total rating of less than 10kW under normal wind conditions;
18A.37.090.A 2. Equipment shall not be located in a front yard setback. All equipment shall comply with setbacks of the zone classification;
18A.37.090.A 3. Height limits for the underlying zone shall apply. The height limit may be exceeded provided the equipment is set back one additional foot for every foot said equipment exceeds the height limitation of the underlying zone classification;
18A.37.090.A 4. Minimum distance between the ground and any protruding blades shall not be less than 15 feet as measured from the lowest point of the arc of the blades;
18A.37.090.A 5. The outer and innermost guy wires must be clearly visible through the use of covers, flagging or other methods to a height of 6 feet above the guy wire anchors;
18A.37.090.A 6. Guy wire anchors must be a minimum of 10 feet from all property lines;
18A.37.090.A 7. Must be in compliance with applicable county, state and federal regulations;
18A.37.090.A 8. Equipment shall not be installed along the major axis of an existing microwave communications link where the operation system is likely to produce an unacceptable level of electromagnetic interference, unless the applicant provides sufficient evidence satisfactory to the Planning Director indicating that the degree of expected interference is insignificant;
18A.37.090.A 9. All equipment shall be located and installed in compliance with the guidelines of the Federal Aviation Regulations if located on property adjacent to an airport runway approach or clearance zone;
18A.37.090.A 10. Noise generated at any property line shall not exceed that allowed under Chapter 8.76 (8.76) PCC; and
18A.37.090.A 11. In urban zones, power lines for a detached unit shall be located underground.

A. The establishment of an ADU shall be subject to the following general requirements:
18A.37.120.A 1. Two ADUs shall be allowed per lot of record as an accessory use within the Urban Growth Area (UGA) and one ADU outside of the UGA in conjunction with any single-family residential structure in all zones where that use is permitted. ADUs shall not be included in density calculations.
18A.37.120.A 2. ADUs shall be permitted as dwelling units attached to, or detached from, the principal structure in any combination.
18A.37.120.A 3. A detached ADU may be any dwelling permitted in the applicable land use classification.
18A.37.120.A 4. New construction of a detached ADU shall not be permitted within the required front, side, or rear yard setback.
18A.37.120.A 5. If an ADU is created by constructing a new detached structure, the building height of the ADU shall not be greater than the principal dwelling's building height, except:
18A.37.120.A 18A.37.120.A.5 a. An ADU may exceed the height of the principal dwelling's building height by one story, when the ADU is above a detached garage.
18A.37.120.A 18A.37.120.A.5 b. No ADU may be limited to a height of less than 24 feet unless the principal dwelling's building height is limited to less than 24 feet.
18A.37.120.A 6. Conversion of an existing detached structure to an ADU shall be permitted within the side or rear yard setback when the structure is located within the UGA.
B. Size. An ADU shall be no greater than 1,000 square feet when located in an Urban Growth Area. When located outside an UGA, the size may increase to 1,250 square feet.
18A.37.120.B 1. Square footage for an ADU shall be calculated based on the interior habitable area of a dwelling unit, including basements and attics, but not including a garage or accessory structure.
C. Design. The following standards shall apply to ADUs located within UGA and rural areas when the separation between the principal structure and proposed accessory dwelling is less than 100 linear feet:
18A.37.120.C 1. Detached ADUs shall be no closer to the front lot line than the closest point to the front lot line of the principal structure, unless the ADU was created by the conversion of a pre-existing structure into the ADU. This provision shall not apply to waterfront lots regulated pursuant to Title 18S (18S) PCC, Development Policies and Regulations – Shorelines.
18A.37.120.C 2. The following setbacks may be applied to ADUs located within the UGA:
18A.37.120.C 18A.37.120.C.2 a. The side/interior and rear yard setbacks may be reduced to 5 feet.
18A.37.120.C 18A.37.120.C.2 b. The rear yard setbacks may be reduced to 2 feet when the ADUs abut an alley.
18A.37.120.C 18A.37.120.C.2 c. On corner lots, the front yard setbacks of the principal structure shall apply for the purposes of siting ADUs.
D. Application. Any owner seeking to establish an ADU shall apply for approval in accordance with established procedures. These procedures shall include:
18A.37.120.D 1. The owner shall apply for a Building Permit for an ADU. A complete application form must demonstrate that all size thresholds and design standards are met.
18A.37.120.D 2. Street improvements shall not be a condition of approval for ADU development.
